Early life

Thug excelled at rapping from an early age, performing freestyle raps during high school and releasing his own mixtapes.

Despite being a local talent, he never imagined becoming a famous rapper and instead focused on street life in his hometown.

It wasn’t until the 1990s when music artist Michael Watts with Swishahouse discovered Thug’s talent. He featured as a guest star on one of their mixtapes, giving him exposure and kickstarting his rapping career.

After working alongside Swishahouse, Thug realized the potential of earning money through selling his mixtapes and decided to pursue it as a career.

He eventually parted ways with Swishahouse on good terms and continued to carve out a successful path in the industry.

Slim Thug Net Worth And Career

He is a hip hop artist who gained recognition after his debut album ‘Already Platinum’ ranked number 2 on Billboard in the US.

Slim started his career with Swishahouse but later formed the band ‘Boss Hogg Outlawz’.

After two years, he made a comeback and collaborated with rapper Lil Keke for ‘The Big Unit’ album.

He also released his solo studio album with Pharrell Williams as his guide and featured guests such as Jay-Z, T.I., and Bun B.

In 2007, he began working on another album, ‘Serve and Collect’, followed by his second studio album, ‘Boss of All Bosses’.

Slim’s success is attributed to his hard work and positive mindset.
