Twenty-Five Twenty-One Season 2 Plot: What could it be about?

Told in two different timelines, the korean drama revolves around two imperfect and different people whose lives cross, just like it was written in destiny. Baek Yi-jin is a hardworking young man trying to rebuild his life after a crisis, while Na Hee-do is a whiz kid fencer whose fencing dream almost deems when she loses her fencing team.

Despite the setbacks the two have been through, they decide to take a different approach to life and build something out of it than wallowing in sorrow. In the present day, however, Na Hee-do’s daughter Kim Min Chae quits ballet and seeks refuge in her grandmother’s house. She chances upon her mother’s diary, and the stories in 1998 are told through her.

Twenty-Five Twenty-One Season 1 Recap:

Twenty-Five Twenty-One is a South Korean drama series that first aired on tvN on February 12, 2022, and was broadcasted every Saturday and Sunday at 21:10 (KST) for a total of 16 episodes. The show revolves around the romantic lives of five characters, spanning from the year 1998 to 2021.

It centers on the love story between Na Hee-Do (played by Kim Tae-Ri) and Back Yi-Jin (played by Nam Joo-Hyuk). Na Hee-Do is a member of her high school fencing team, but due to the South Korean financial crisis, the team is disbanded. Despite the challenges, she manages to become a member of the sabre fencing national team.

The financial crisis also affects Back Yi-Jin’s father’s business, causing them to go bankrupt and forcing Back Yi-Jin to transition from a life of luxury to a more humble existence. While pursuing his studies, he takes on part-time jobs such as delivering newspapers and eventually becomes a sports reporter for a broadcasting network.
